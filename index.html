The Journey of Jenna Ortega

Jenna Marie Ortega (born September 27, 2002) is an American actress whose career began at the age of nine. From her early breakout in the CW dramedy series Jane the Virgin to her status as a global icon, Jenna has consistently chosen roles that challenge expectations.

Beyond her acting prowess, she is a published author of the book "It's All Love" and a dedicated advocate for representation in Hollywood, using her platform to champion her Mexican and Puerto Rican heritage.

Netflix Global Phenomenon

Wednesday

In this Tim Burton reimagining, Jenna portrays Wednesday Addams during her years as a student at Nevermore Academy. Her performance, including the now-viral self-choreographed dance, helped the show become one of the most-watched English-language series in Netflix history. She perfectly balances the character's cold exterior with a sharp, detective-like intelligence.
Warner Bros. Pictures

Beetlejuice Beetlejuice

Stepping into the cult classic sequel as Astrid Deetz, the rebellious daughter of Lydia Deetz (Winona Ryder). Jenna brings a grounded, modern energy to the whimsical and chaotic world of the Afterlife. Her collaboration with Tim Burton continues to define her career as a "Scream Queen" and dramatic powerhouse of the 21st century.
Paramount Horror

Scream VI

Reprising her role as Tara Carpenter, Jenna takes center stage as a survivor facing Ghostface in the sprawling streets of New York City. Her performance in the Scream franchise has been praised for its emotional depth, proving she can lead a massive horror franchise with both vulnerability and immense strength.

Personal Roots

Family & Origins

Jenna Marie Ortega was raised in the Coachella Valley, California. She is the fourth of six children, growing up in a close-knit household of Mexican and Puerto Rican descent. She often speaks about how her parents sacrificed a lot to help her pursue acting, and how her large family keeps her grounded despite her massive international fame.
